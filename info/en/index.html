Using the Jacuzzi

  • It is crucial to the water quality for you all to shower before entering the jacuzzi.
    Even if you "feel" clean, there are always loose skin particles and residues on the skin that are not good for the water and filters.
    • Hair, sweat, makeup, soap residue, sunscreen, etc. are bad for the water and filters.
    +
    • We know, it's tempting to skip it, please don't :-)
  • Bring a cloth. Upon arrival + occasionally, we usually bring a cloth (not the kitchen cloth), there should be one together with the other jacuzzi items, to wipe along the waterline where things typically settle.
    Then the filters should catch until next time.

  • Rinse / wash your feet before getting in.
  • The cover, we usually place standing at an angle on the side either from where the wind blows or as a privacy shield, most often it stands as in the picture.
  • If you need to adjust the temperature, do so by pressing the "Tmp" button.
    The first press shows the current setting.
    Each new press changes the temperature by 0.5 degrees.
    Press the "Light" button to "save" the temperature (or do nothing, the selected temperature will also be saved).
    • Each time you have saved and press "Tmp" again (after it stops blinking), it switches between increasing or decreasing the temperature.

  • Put the cover on between each use.
    • Small flies etc. also like to bathe.
  • Inside: Keep the wooden floor dry after bathing/showering, if you walk through the cabin wet, find old towels in the bathroom drawer which can be placed/used on the floor. The point is to avoid leaving water that will stain the wooden floor.

  • There is chlorine in the water. Perfect water is not always easy, many factors are involved, the short version based on experience is:
  • When you are done:
    • Add 1 teaspoon of dissolved chlorine to the water. (Use the liter measure.)
  • After about 3-4 baths (typically every other day +):
    • Add 1 small teaspoon of "pH-plus" powder directly to the water.
    P.S. Chlorine dissipates over (a short) time, so a little too much is ok, while the pH-level does not decrease by itself. You can either measure or feel the pH; if it gets a bit high, the water can feel soapy and the bubbles do not burst as easy and you will get a kind of foam bath (it is not dangerous).
    (and the body waste from those bathing settles on the bubbles and the pool edge instead of in the filter =) )
    If you are unsure or think it is a bit too much, use a pH strip and test the water.
  • Every other day (provided you have bathed both days):
    • Also add 1 small teaspoon of "Clear pool" directly to the water. (This prevents biofilm in the bath.)
    That's it, done.

    There is still a bit more to know :-)
  • The chlorine should be dissolved before it is poured into the water, we use the liter-measure that is together with the jacuzzi items (at the bottom of the gray shelf by the entrance door).
  • Start with water in the liter measure, preferably take water directly from the jacuzzi, then add the powder, stir until most of it is dissolved.
  • Then pour the chlorine water around the edge of the bath or near the intake of the filters (when the pump is running).
  • If the water quality is visibly reduced, use instead: 2 teaspoons of chlorine.
    + consider 1 teaspoon of “Clear pool” (added directly to the bath, not in the chlorine solution).
  • Let all three bubbles run for 8 minutes, preferably with the lid partially on.
    (I personally place the lid completely over but let it rest on the liter measure so that plenty of air comes in/out)

  • P.S. There are also "pH strips" to check the pH value which should be around 7.0.
    We have never needed to lower the pH value, only raise it, which is why only the "ph-plus" version is available.
    If you want to adjust the pH value, it takes a little time in the sense that the water needs to be well mixed and it is important not to add too much "plus" at once. The pH-scale is not linear and you can quickly add too much.
    1 teaspoon or a little less is what we usually use, do not use more than that at a time, it can be added directly to the pool as powder or dissolved, then the water needs to be well mixed before measuring again.
    Tip: The first day at the cabin when a bath is done: Measure the pH, if it is below 7.0, add 1 teaspoon of "plus" (directly in the bath) + chlorine (dissolved) as mentioned above, then it will be well mixed by the next morning.
